I think that smartphones don't need to be used in the classrooms at all. There is no need and it is distracting. In one of my classes, people put their phone in front of their laptop screens (so it can't be seen) and wear wireless earphones (so they're less noticable) to watch Netflix or something unrelated. I think it is disrespectful and a waste of education. It is distracting to people around and to themselves. School is a place to learn and phones make it worse. Some people can't afford phones and may not have a phone so it's not great to them. Using phones during class is irresponsible of a students' own learning. Even if they are told not to, they'll do it anyway.

It can be useful in the sense that students check their timetable or time, but there are usually clocks in the classroom and we have two classes and then a break. In the break, they can check their next two classes and remember those instead of looking at class time. It is more of a distraction than a help. School is where you're meant to learn. Phones do the oppposite of helping learn. In this day, a lot of poeple have social media and check it constatnly. This is something they can do during intervals or before and after school. In my opininion, soical media is boring and pointless and it's not important. I once did this thing where I took a break from social media for a week and it felt like a real break. A break from that world. Sometimes we get caught up in the world of phones and internet, we forget about the real world. The world is dying and there's no denying it. The next generation, those at school, are the ones who are gonna have to save it before it's too late. How can we do that if we can't even focus in class?
